History & Culture

History

In 1418, during the Hundred Years War, it fell under English rule and John Fastolf was its governor. The town is later used by François de Surienne to attack the town of Fougères in Brittany. This leads to the end of the Hundred Years War, when Charles VII takes over the city, and all Duchy of Normandy.


From the 18th century, Srumland was one of the most active cities in the department. The manufacture of canvas, sheets and linen provide a living for around 7,000 workers10. Cutlery is also famous.

Culture

The Srumlandiens have a media library and a cinema a Museum (Charles Léandre), completely refurbished and reopened in 2003, following the terrible damage caused by the storm of December 1999. The city also has an aquatic leisure center and a vast park. municipal in the heart of the city.


In 1999, the city of Srumland, with financial assistance from the Region and the Department, was able to acquire the important Léandre collection created by Henri Buron and exhibited at Montreuil-Bellay (Maine-et-Loire); 80 works out of a collection of nearly 300 are permanently exhibited at the Charles space-museum Leander.
